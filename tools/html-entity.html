HTML Entity Encoder / Decoder

Encode special characters (like < > &) to HTML entities, or decode entities back to plain text.

About this Tool

Our HTML Entity Encoder/Decoder ensures that special characters in your text are safely displayed on the web without breaking your HTML structure. Convert reserved characters like angle brackets and ampersands into their corresponding HTML entities, or decode entities back to regular characters when reading raw code.

How to Use

  1. Paste your standard text or HTML-encoded text into the input field.
  2. Click "Encode" to convert special characters (like quotes and brackets) into HTML entities.
  3. Click "Decode" to translate an already encoded string back into readable characters.
  4. Click the "Copy" button to grab the result.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do I need to encode HTML entities?

Encoding prevents characters like `<` or `>` from breaking your website's layout or triggering unintended code execution (XSS attacks).

Which characters get encoded?

This tool specifically encodes the characters that have special meaning in HTML, including ampersands, quotation marks, apostrophes, and angle brackets.

Is this tool safe for sensitive data?

Absolutely. Your text is processed entirely within your browser environment. Nothing is sent to external servers.
